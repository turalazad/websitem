Moon

The Moon, Earth’s only natural satellite, orbits 384,400 kilometers away. Its surface features craters, mountains, and lava plains. With no atmosphere, it experiences extreme temperatures. The Moon’s gravity influences Earth’s tides and rhythms. Always showing the same face, it inspires myths, art, and exploration, symbolizing mystery and discovery.

Mars

Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is known as the Red Planet due to its iron-rich, rust-colored soil. It has the largest volcano, Olympus Mons, and a deep canyon, Valles Marineris. With a thin atmosphere of carbon dioxide, Mars hosts frozen water, fueling hopes of potential past or future life.

Sun

The Sun, a massive ball of hot plasma, is the center of our solar system. It provides light and heat, sustaining life on Earth. Composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, nuclear fusion powers its energy. The Sun’s gravity holds planets in orbit, making it essential for the stability of our system.
